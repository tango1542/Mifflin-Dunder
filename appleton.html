Appleton, WI Manufacturing Plant

In the late 1940's, Mifflin Dunder could not keep up with the demand for paper. A new factory was opened up in Appleton, WI, with its plentiful workforce and ample access to the logging resources of the North Woods of Wisconsin. The facility in Appleton has slowly taken over the Minneapolis facility in terms of production, finally surpassing the output of Minneapolis in 1982.

Today, the Mifflin Dunder mill in Appleton is a stalwart of the community, and it is ingrained in the fabric of this great city. The facility continues to add on even with the advent of digital technologies that are causing a decline worldwide in the paper printing industry.
